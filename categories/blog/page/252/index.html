published on June 13, 2024
TIME March 1, 1943 12:00 AM GMT-4 “How do you pronounce your name?” a reporter asked Mme. Chiang. Her answer: There is a Chinese way and an American way. In American, it is chee-ang. In Mandarin, now the official dialect of China, it is approximately John with a g on the end. Other Mandarin pronunciations: Kuomintang (the Party): gwaw-min-dahng. Meiling: may-ling.
