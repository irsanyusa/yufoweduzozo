So You Think You Can Dance finished up its most recent season earlier this year, and now it’s time for hopefuls to audition for Season 18. Online registration is now open, and there are a few things to keep in mind when signing up.

How to Audition for So You Think You Can Dance

The registration form for SYTYCD Season 18 is currently open. Be sure to read the eligibility requirements. This year’s requirements include providing proof of identity and citizenship, as well as proof of vaccination against Covid-19. One you’ve agreed to those requirements, you can fill in your information.

Hopefuls need to be between 18 and 30 years old to audition for the show. You’ll be asked to provide basic information such as name and date of birth, as well as if you’ve auditioned for other talent shows in the past.

You’ll need to choose a style of dance to audition in. The choices include Hip Hop, Contemporary, Jazz, Ballroom/Latin, Tap, Ballet, and Specialty. The form also asks hopefuls to provide interesting information about themselves and to upload a recent headshot.

When signing up, you’ll also be asked to share a recent solo performance video in your chosen style of dance. The form asks for only YouTube and Vimeo links. Videos must not be longer than 90 seconds.

When Will the New Season Premiere?

A release date for Season 18 of So You Think You Can Dance has yet to be announced. However, if it follows the same schedule as the most recent season, it could premiere in May of next year and air a finale in August.

The show returned this year after a three-year hiatus due to the Covid-19 pandemic, with new judges JoJo Siwa, Stephen “tWitch” Boss, and Matthew Morrison. Not long into the season, Morrison exited the show and was replaced by Leah Remini.

Alexis Warr was announced as the winner of Season 18. She’s already joined the troupe of Dancing with the Stars, and she recently filled in for Daniella Karagach to dance with Joseph Baena on Elvis Night.
